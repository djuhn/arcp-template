ARCP Requirements

Northwest School of Anaesthesia

Work in Progress

Completion of training in anaesthesia involves satisfying the requirements of the North West School of Anaesthesia, the Royal College of Anaesthetists and the General Medical Council. The introduction of both a new Curriculum and an electronic Portfolio has led to changes in the way trainees collect and present evidence for review at ARCP.

The aim of this document is to explicitly state the requirements for a satisfactory outcome at ARCP. This benefits trainees, who have a clear blueprint of their requirements; educational supervisors, who are able to offer consistent guidance; and the ARCP panel, who are presented evidence in a consistent format.

It may well be that in the future the ePortfolio is closed to changes by trainees in the days immediately prior to their ARCP date to allow the panel members to review their evidence before meeting. It is strongly recommended that trainees keep their ePortfolio up-to-date and stay on top of these requirements.

Audit/Clinical Governance Summary - TICK

Items

There must be evidence in your ePortfolio that you have actively participated and contributed to one of the following:
  • Audit with recognisable standards (eg. audit recipe book)
  • Re-audit to close the audit loop

CME Points

Items

  1. Summary document totalling internal and external points - template.
  2. CME/CPD certificates, certificates and records of attendence.
Format
PDFs, Word documents or Spreadsheet
Titles
  1. Summary: Named with the year & month of your ARCP in the format:
    Evidence - CME Summary - 2012 August
  2. Individual documents: Named with year & month activity undertaken in the format:
    Evidence - Certificate - 2012 June - Title of Course
Frequency
One summary per ARCP period.
CPD Point Requirement
25 internal and 25 external CPD/CME points per year.
Guidance
Internal CME is anything undertaken within your base hospital: eg. attending teaching & audit meetings, formal teaching sessions of junior trainees/medical students (note this does not include informal theatre or ward-based teaching). It also includes any online learning activity with UK accredited CME/CPD points. Do we want to specify this? RCOA approved only? For ICU people, other bodies may be appropriate... Do we take this out altogether?

External CME is any regional or national teaching or course undertaken away from your base hospital.

Points are awarded as follows:
  • 1 point per hour of activity
  • Maximum of 5 points per whole day
  • Maximum of 3 points per half day
For consultants, the RCOA places limits on the number of points that can be claimed for attending or teaching courses (for more details see ). However, it has been decided not to apply these limits to North West trainees. You can, therefore, list external points for ALS, APLS and ATLS in the same year as a trainee in the North West, something that would not be permitted as a consultant.
